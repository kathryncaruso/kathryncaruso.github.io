## Overview

Eastern hemlock (_Tsuga canadensis_) forests across the southeastern United States are declining due to infestation by hemlock woolly adelgid (_Adelges tsugae_). This project investigated how hemlock decline affects ectomycorrhizal (ECM) colonization and growth of red oak (_Quercus rubra_) seedlings — a species expected to replace hemlock in many transitioning forests.

## Key Findings

Published in _American Midland Naturalist_ (2021), this work demonstrated the complex belowground consequences of hemlock loss, contributing to our understanding of how invasive species cascade through forest ecosystems.
