Carbon Source Growth Assay

Protocol for determining preferred carbon source for cold-adapted ureolytic bacterial isolates via OD600 growth curves.

📄 Written Protocol · 🧪 Interactive Diagram →

Overview

This protocol describes a growth assay to determine preferred carbon source for cold-adapted ureolytic bacterial isolates. Isolates are grown in liquid urea-based media with different carbon sources, and growth is monitored via OD600 measurements over ~3 weeks.

Purpose: Compare growth of glacier-derived bacterial isolates on different carbon substrates (glucose, starch, acetate) to identify preferred carbon source for future MICP (microbially-induced calcium carbonate precipitation) applications in permafrost environments.

Experimental Conditions

Condition Carbon Source Concentration Notes

3 Succinate 0.5 g/L  

Experimental Design

  • 4 isolates × 3 conditions × 3 replicates = 36 inoculated tubes
  • 4 uninoculated media blanks (1 per condition) = 4 blank tubes
  • Total: 40 tubes

Carbon source concentrations are matched on a molar carbon basis to 0.5 g/L glucose (~16.67 mmol C/L).

1. Pre-experiment Preparation

Timeline: Combustion and autoclaving must be completed before media preparation day. Combustion takes several hours including cool-down.

1.1 Combust Glassware

Combustion (dry heat sterilization) removes all organic residues and ensures tubes are free of contaminants.

  1. Wrap glass culture tubes (×40 minimum + spares) loosely in aluminum foil
  2. Place in muffle furnace / combustion oven
  3. Combust at 450–500°C for 5 hours
  4. Allow to cool completely inside the oven before removing
  5. Keep tubes wrapped/covered until use

⚠️ Do not combust any plastic components (caps, stickers, etc.)

1.2 Autoclave Tube Closures and Equipment

Autoclave all non-glass items that will contact sterile media or cultures at 121°C, 15 psi, 15–20 min.

2. Preparing Media

Prepare 4 separate base media (one per carbon source condition), each at sufficient volume.

pH target 6.8 ± 0.2 6.8 ± 0.2 6.8 ± 0.2 6.8 ± 0.2

2.2 Volume Planning

Prepare 50 mL of final medium per condition (200 mL total). Final medium = 90% base medium + 10% urea stock.

2.3–2.5 Preparation Steps

  1. Weigh components, dissolve in ~80% target volume
  2. Adjust pH to 6.8 ± 0.2 individually for each condition
  3. Autoclave base medium (121°C, 15 psi, 15–20 min, liquid cycle)
  4. Prepare 20% w/v urea stock separately (filter sterilize, do NOT autoclave)
  5. Aseptically combine 90% base + 10% urea stock

3. Inoculum Standardization

  1. Select individual colony from R2A agar plate for each isolate
  2. Document colony morphology
  3. Transfer colony into 1 mL glucose condition medium
  4. Vortex gently, measure OD600
  5. Dilute each suspension to consistent target starting OD (e.g., 0.01–0.05)
  6. Inoculate experimental tubes with standardized suspension

4. Tube Labeling

Format: Isolate-CarbonSource-Replicate

Examples: GG27B-Gluc-1, GNP14-Ace-3, BLK-Star

5. Incubation

  • Temperature: 15°C
  • Agitation: Rotating shaker
  • Duration: ~21 days (3 weeks)

6. OD600 Measurements

Readings taken Monday / Wednesday / Friday for 3 weeks.

Measurement Procedure

  1. Remove tubes from shaker
  2. Mix by inversion (2–3×)
  3. Blank spectrophotometer with corresponding uninoculated blank
  4. Read OD600 directly in glass culture tubes
  5. Record readings + visual observations (color, flocculation, biofilm)
  6. Return tubes to shaker promptly

Consistency: Use the same spectrophotometer, same tube orientation, and same technique each time.

7. Endpoint Measurements (~Day 21)

  • Final OD600 reading
  • Endpoint pH — compare to initial 6.8; increase toward alkaline suggests urease activity
  • Visual assessment — photograph tubes grouped by condition; note biofilm, pellicle, or precipitate

Phenol Red Color Reference

pH Range Color Interpretation
< 6.8 Yellow More acidic than starting pH
6.8 (start) Yellow-orange Starting pH range
6.8–8.2 Yellow-orange to pink Gradual alkalinization
> 8.2 Pink-red Alkaline; consistent with ureolysis

Notes & Reminders

  • Work in biosafety cabinet for all aseptic steps
  • Always include uninoculated blanks (never skip)
  • Starch blank is especially important — starch can contribute to baseline turbidity
  • Keep original R2A plates as backup at 4°C
  • If contamination is suspected, note but do not discard — streak onto R2A at endpoint to check purity
